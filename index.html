If I was to order soup from a restaurant and saw when they placed the soup in front of me that it had a fly floating in it, I would notify the waiter and, I expect, be served a new bowl of soup. The Boy Scouts of America like many large organizations has had its fair share of accusations against it. However, those accusations are to me like a fly in the soup. I don't know how much of the soup has been contaminated and so am tempted to refuse the entire thing. But what if this is my favorite soup? In fact, I like this soup so much that throwing a quantity away feels like a travesty. I might try to rescue the soup by simply removing the fly and enjoying what remains. For some, it seems that though they liked the boys scouts well enough it isn't worthy of saving. Others loved the boy scouts and wish to preserve the good in it while throwing out what is objectionable.
